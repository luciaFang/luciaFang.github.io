FALL 2022 | UX DESIGN

Our website, Rate My TA, is designed specifically for CMU students who want to share their opinions on the quality of teaching assistants in their courses. It provides a platform for students to leave comments and select labels that reflect the teaching style of the TAs. Other students can view past ratings, compare them, and choose a TA that fits their learning style. By using this platform, TAs can also use the ratings and comments to reflect on their performance and strive to improve. Rate My TA is a valuable tool for students and TAs alike, facilitating a more effective and fulfilling learning experience.

Specific, pre-determined categories/labels for rating TAs (such as "friendly" or "easy explanation").
Ability to leave anonymous comments alongside selecting the categories mentioned above.
Ability to report/flag comments for moderators to review if they are overly critical or rude.
User verification through logging in with your Andrew ID, ensuring the system is not abused by outsiders or non-enrolled individuals.
Ability to view past labels and comments for the TAs of the courses you are taking and compare them with each other.

Prototyping & Wireframing

Throughout the development of our project, we made several significant improvements to enhance the user experience. In particular, we added functionality to automatically populate text boxes when clicked, included a loading screen to indicate progress after logging in, and revised usernames for commenters to better ensure anonymity. These changes helped to create an authentic and engaging prototype.

We also benefited greatly from feedback received from Professor Uchidiuno, who helped us shape the project from its early stages. One key piece of advice was to reconsider our initial plan of using a 1-5 star rating system for TAs, which could potentially impact their mental health. Instead, we adopted a set of pre-determined positive labels, which allowed us to better accommodate the needs of all users. This experience taught us a valuable lesson about the importance of considering multiple perspectives during the design process and prioritizing the user experience.

In addition to the screens depicted in the images above, our prototype includes several interactive features that enhance the user experience. For instance, clicking on any text box - such as the "Comment" box on the sixth screen or the "Andrew ID" field on the second screen - triggers the display of a keyboard, enabling the user to enter text easily. Similarly, clicking on the "Report" button beside a comment on the fifth screen generates a pop-up message thanking the user for their feedback and informing them that the comment will be reviewed by a moderator. Following our conversation with Professor Uchidiuno, we decided to make an important change to our approach to rating TAs. Rather than relying on a 5-star system, which could create an unhealthy culture of comparison, we opted to implement a "key labels" section for each TA. This feature enables users to select from a predetermined list of positive labels (screen 6), highlighting the specific areas in which each TA excels. Although not shown in the paper prototype, we plan to display the highest-scoring label for each TA on the summary screen (screen 4) to help students identify TAs who possess the qualities they find most valuable when seeking answers to their questions. Overall, these features make our prototype more engaging and user-friendly, enhancing the overall user experience. To enhance the overall user experience, we incorporated Gestalt principles, such as similarity on the course pages and proximity on the individual TA page. We also changed the color scheme to a reddish-brown hue, which is similar to the color used in official CMY web services. This modification gives the prototype a more authentic university feel and enhances its connection to the broader campus community.
